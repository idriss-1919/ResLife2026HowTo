University Apartments South

Apartment Resident Assistant (ARA)

The ARA is a live-in student leader in University Apartments South (UAS) who helps create a community where every resident feels welcomed, connected, and supported.

Serving graduate students, students with families, and postdoctoral residents, the ARA acts as a community builder, responder, teammate, and institutional representative, making UAS feel like home in both everyday interactions and critical moments.

What you'll need

To be eligible for the role, you'll need to meet the following requirements.

  • Be a full-time UCLA student
  • Maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.3 (or meet graduate program standards)
  • Have lived in a UCLA Housing community for at least one academic year
  • Be eligible to work in the U.S. and complete a background check
  • Hold a valid U.S. driver's license

What we're looking for

Traits, not credentials. The strongest ARAs bring a shared set of qualities, and we'll support you in building the skills needed for the role.

Genuine care for people and community Strong communication and active listening Reliability and follow-through Sound judgment Time management and organization Willingness to learn and grow Ability to work collaboratively with others Alignment with UCLA and Residential Life values

What you'll do

Four core areas of the role: community, response, operations, and standards.

Build community

01

Create an environment where residents feel they belong.

  • Plan and implement weekly community-building engagements
  • Welcome residents and set clear community expectations
  • Build relationships and maintain a visible, supportive presence
  • Facilitate roommate conversations and conflict resolution

Support & respond

02

Be part of the team residents rely on when things come up.

  • Participate in a weekly overnight duty rotation
  • Respond to incidents in line with established protocols
  • Support appropriate follow-up after incidents
  • Connect residents to academic and campus resources

Operate effectively

03

Support the day-to-day operations of the community.

  • Attend weekly staff meetings (Mondays, 6–9 PM) and biweekly supervisor meetings
  • Support move-in, welcome efforts, and community transitions
  • Complete administrative tasks and communications as expected
  • Participate in training and ongoing development

Uphold standards

04

Maintain a safe, respectful, and accountable living environment.

  • Know, uphold, and enforce university and housing policies
  • Document incidents accurately and in a timely manner
  • Maintain professionalism, confidentiality, and integrity
  • Contribute to a coordinated, 24/7 residential support system

Training & development

You'll be set up to succeed and supported through the year. That includes mandatory summer training, ongoing development across the academic year, and all University-required training. Each cycle pairs operational protocol with personal growth so you can lead with confidence.

Summer training begins about three weeks before classes start and runs for eleven days, eight hours a day, including a weekend retreat. It's intensive, and it's how the team builds the shared foundation you'll rely on all year.

What to expect

The practical shape of the role: dates, commitments, and what to plan for.

  • The role is a one academic year commitment.
  • You'll start on Monday, August 31, 2026 and wrap up on June 30, 2027.
  • Summer ARA roles are available starting July 1, 2027 for those who want to stay involved.
  • If you're considering other jobs, internships, or assistantships, check in with your supervisor first to ensure everything is aligned for your success.

What you'll gain

The skills and experiences you'll carry well beyond the role.

  • Leadership and communication that translate across any path
  • A strong foundation in teamwork and collaboration
  • Confidence in decision-making and crisis response
  • Real-world experience navigating complex and dynamic situations
  • Skills in conflict mediation and resolution
  • The ability to build and sustain community
  • Lasting relationships that grow into a lifelong network of colleagues, mentors, and support
  • The opportunity to make a meaningful impact on others' lives
Why this role matters

More than programs or duty shifts.

This role is the connective tissue of UAS. It's the difference between a place where residents live and a place where they belong.

  • Creating spaces where people feel they belong
  • Supporting residents through important moments
  • Building a community rooted in connection, care, and accountability.

You help create the experience people remember, and the environment where they succeed.
