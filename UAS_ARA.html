Apartment Resident Assistant (ARA)

Section 01

What this role is about

The ARA is a live-in student leader in University Apartments South (UAS) who helps create a welcoming, connected, and supportive community. This role blends community builder, crisis and emergency responder, institutional representative, and team member. Each conversation, duty shift, and program is an opportunity to make UAS feel like home.

Section 02

What you'll do

Four core responsibilities: community, response, standards, and operations. Together they shape every week of the role.

Build community

01

Create an environment where residents feel they belong.

  • Welcome residents and set clear community expectations
  • Plan and implement weekly community-building programs and engagement
  • Create virtual connection spaces (bulletin boards, Slack, GroupMe)
  • Facilitate roommate discussions, agreements, and conflict resolution
  • Build relationships so residents feel known, connected, and supported

Support & respond

02

Be part of the team residents rely on when things come up.

  • Participate weekly in a scheduled duty rotation with defined shifts
  • Respond to incidents (lockouts, wellness concerns, emergencies)
  • Assess situations: act, refer, or escalate as appropriate
  • Connect residents to appropriate campus resources
  • Support follow-up after incidents with professional staff
  • Contribute to a coordinated, timely, thoughtful response system

Uphold community standards

03

Maintain a safe, respectful, and accountable living environment.

  • Know, uphold, and enforce university and housing policies
  • Address policy violations and community concerns appropriately
  • Document incidents accurately and on time
  • Maintain confidentiality and professionalism
  • Balance care and accountability in all interactions

Help things run smoothly

04

Support the day-to-day operations of the community.

  • Attend weekly staff and biweekly supervisor meetings
  • Support move-in, welcome efforts, and community transitions
  • Follow up on common concerns (maintenance, noise, policy)
  • Help residents navigate systems and know who to contact
  • Complete administrative tasks, reports, and communications as expected
Section 03

What we're looking for

Traits, not credentials. The strongest ARAs share a few qualities, and we'll help you build the rest.

Genuine care for people & community Strong communication & listening Reliability & follow-through Time management Sound judgment Willingness to learn & grow Represents UCLA & ResLife values
Section 04

Role expectations & accountability

The ARA role carries priority commitments. You'll have clear schedules, expectations, and support, and we'll expect consistent follow-through in return.

  1. This role carries priority commitments and responsibility.
  2. Outside commitments must be approved and not conflict with ARA responsibilities.
  3. You are expected to be a positive role model, both on and off duty.
  4. While not on call 24/7, you remain a visible and influential presence.
  5. You will have clear duty schedules, expectations, and support systems.
  6. You are expected to document, communicate, and follow through consistently.
Section 05

Training & development

You'll be set up to succeed and supported through the year. Participate in mandatory summer training and ongoing development throughout the year. Each training cycle pairs operational protocol with personal growth so you can lead with confidence.

Section 06

What you'll gain

The skills and experiences you'll carry well past the role.

  • Leadership and communication that translate across any path
  • A strong foundation in teamwork and collaboration
  • Confidence in decision-making and crisis response
  • Real-world experience navigating complex and dynamic situations
  • Skills in conflict mediation and resolution
  • The ability to build and sustain community
  • Lasting relationships that evolve into a lifelong network of colleagues, mentors, and support systems
  • An opportunity to meaningfully impact others' lives
Why this role matters

More than programs or duty shifts.

This role is the connective tissue of UAS. It's the difference between a place where residents live and a place where they belong.

  • Creating spaces where people feel they belong
  • Supporting residents through important moments
  • Building a community rooted in care, accountability, and connection

You help create the experience people remember, and the environment where they succeed.
