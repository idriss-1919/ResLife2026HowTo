Training structure

Two phases.

1

Phase one

Department Training

Everyone learns together before specializing. Same language, same expectations, same approach — across all three pillars. This is where the foundation is built.

All staff · Shared baseline across Welcomes · Connects · Responds

2

Phase two

Unit Training

Each team builds role-specific knowledge under each pillar, culminating in a capstone (Unit 399) where staff practice decisions through real-world scenarios.

Role-specific · Specialization builds on the shared base · Capstone: Unit 399

"Department training builds the foundation. Unit training builds the rooms. The architecture holds it all together."

How every session runs

The LED Method

Three phases that move participants from foundational knowledge to real-world application to expert insight. Every live training session at ResLife follows this approach.

L
Learn

Self-paced pre-work — completed 24 hours before the session

Articles, readings, and pre-recorded videos covering key concepts. Real-world scenarios and reflection questions to arrive prepared.

E
Engage

Facilitator-led live session — reinforce, align, and set the stage

Welcome and recap, then a deep-dive with real-world examples. Polls, knowledge checks, and small group discussion — building on pre-work, not repeating it.

D
Deepen

Expert panel + open Q&A — real-world insight beyond the curriculum

Guided panel discussion with 4–6 pre-prepared questions, then open Q&A. Wrap-up summarizes key insights and issues a challenge to apply learning.
