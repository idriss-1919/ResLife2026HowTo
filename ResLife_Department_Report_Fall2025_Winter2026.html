Executive Summary

This report synthesizes quarterly reports from all nine UCLA Residential Life units for Fall 2025 and Winter 2026 — the first two quarters of the 2025–2026 academic year. Together, these quarters represent the department's full operational arc from Move-In through the end of winter academics, covering more than 19,000 residents on and around the Hill.

Fall 2025 was defined by a historic scale of opening operations, record programming attendance, new technology system launches (SmartSuite), significant staffing transitions, and foundational infrastructure work (The Den construction, Six Peak project). Winter 2026 shifted toward sustained service delivery under heightened complexity: incident volumes hit record highs in Residential Education, financial throughput grew double digits, and multiple units navigated AD-level vacancies while maintaining program quality.

Staffing Transitions

Multiple AD vacancies across Conduct and AFYE; Finance lost Ryan Chen; L&I onboarded new LDC Mina Santamaria; LLC Coordinator transitioned from Ella Hokanson to Danielle Espinosa.

Technology Modernization

SmartSuite fully launched in Finance and Residential Education for duty logging; digital systems improving workflow across Central Services and Conduct units.

Record-Setting Scale

19,886 residents moved in during Fall; Learning Centers hit 7,000+ visits in Fall; 1,020 RA applications received in Winter; Finance 29.7% transaction increase in Fall.

Community Safety & Response

CORE co-response launched; Saxon flood + Hedrick vandalism displaced 56 residents in Winter; Conduct managing higher complexity cases despite lower volume; UCPD weekly sync established.

Student Engagement

10,000+ Westwood Block Party; 700+ Noche de Cultura; 425 Winter In-Service; FAB set record proposal volumes across both quarters; PEM delivered 50+ programs in Fall alone.

Capital & Infrastructure

The Den completed in Winter 2026 after multi-quarter build-out; Six Peak secured 396 beds; 205 Hub received new décor; Makerspace expansion drove $27,700 in student savings.
