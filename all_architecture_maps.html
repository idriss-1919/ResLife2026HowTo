System Design
How the Training Is Designed
Every year, we train approximately 700 staff members, including student staff, professional staff, and faculty-in-residence. Each role is unique, but all of them shape the resident experience. To prepare them well, we use a shared training architecture built on two phases, designed around a common framework.
Framework
The Three Pillars
All training is organized around three core areas called pillars. These three pillars represent what ResLife does at its core.

Architecture
The Two Training Phases
Think of the training architecture like building a house. The architecture is the blueprint. Department training lays the foundation. Unit training builds the rooms on top.

Training Architecture
Phase 1
Department Training
What Everyone Learns Together
Before any staff member specializes, they go through a shared curriculum with the whole department.
Why Department Training?
  • Everyone speaks the same language
  • Everyone understands the same expectations
  • Everyone approaches the work the same way
What Does It Look Like?
The curriculum covers all three pillars and within each one follows the PRO progression:
100 Series
Philosophy
The why
200 Series
Roles
The who
300 Series
Operations
The how
✓
By the end of department training, every staff member shares a common language, aligned expectations, and a unified approach to the work.
Phase 2
Unit Training
Where Learning Gets Specific
After the shared foundation, each unit (team) builds role-specific knowledge and then applies it to their specific context. ResLife is made up of specialized teams, each with its own focus area, staff population, and operational responsibilities.
How Unit Training Is Structured
Unit 100 Series
Your Unit
Purpose, people, projects, and patterns
Unit 200 Series
Your Role
Responsibilities, expectations, and relationships
Unit 300 Series
Your Operations
Systems, processes, and practices
Unit 399
Capstone
Practicing decisions and demonstrating judgment
✓
By the end of unit training, each staff member is connected, confident, and prepared to lead within their role.
In Summary
Three Pillars
Define what we do: Welcomes, Connects, Responds.
PRO Progression
Defines the why, the who, and the how.
Department Training
Builds a shared foundation for all staff.
Unit Training
Adds the role-specific knowledge and practice each team needs.
