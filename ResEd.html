UCLA Residential Life · Senior Leadership

Director of Residential Education, On-Campus Housing

About UCLA Residential Life

At UCLA Residential Life, we lead communities where every resident feels welcomed, connected, and supported. We serve more than 15,000 undergraduates in our residence halls and 12,000 undergraduate and graduate students in our apartments and family housing. Our work is carried out by 700+ student staff, 100 professional staff, and 20 Faculty-in-Residence, together making UCLA a place where community thrives.

Position Overview

The Director of Residential Education, On-Campus Housing is a full-time, live-in professional responsible for implementing a comprehensive residential life program that encourages student engagement, academic success, and community building for the 15,000+ undergraduate residents living in UCLA’s on-campus residence halls. The Director plays a significant role in advancing UCLA’s mission, Student Affairs’ strategic initiatives, and the mission, vision, and values of the Office of Residential Life.

Reporting to the Executive Director of Residential Life and First Year Experience, the Director of Residential Education, On-Campus Housing provides senior-level leadership for Residential Education programs and services that support the On-Campus Housing residential community, UCLA’s first-year-heavy traditional residence hall environment, where the majority of incoming Bruins begin their college experience. Residential Education staff in On-Campus Housing, who report to the Director, provide a broad range of student affairs and student development functions to create safe, supportive, and inclusive living-learning communities.

The Director is responsible to respond to life-threatening emergencies and after-hours crises and will live on campus and oversee all emergency and crisis response of the On-Campus Housing Residential Education Team. The Director serves as a primary administrator-on-call within a department rotation, providing 24/7 emergency response coordination through an on-call system in partnership with the Director of Residential Education, Apartment Housing.

Serving as a member of the Residential Life Executive Leadership Team, the Director functions with a high degree of autonomy in multiple roles, and is primarily responsible for independently exercising authority and making decisions in regard to On-Campus Housing Residential Education based on broad goals established collaboratively with the Executive Director and peers in the department.

The Director is responsible for: strategic planning and implementation of an integrated Residential Education program serving On-Campus Housing’s first-year and continuing undergraduate population; direct supervision of 4 full-time Assistant Directors who are formally evaluated annually; coordinating the efforts of On-Campus Housing’s residential areas and integrating with Student Conduct, Leadership and Involvement, and First Year Experience units; overseeing an On-Campus Housing Residential Education budget of approximately $5 million; performing management-level assignments for On-Campus Housing, including, but not limited to, human resource and performance management, chairing and representing the department on a variety of standing and ad-hoc committees, developing operational enhancements, research analysis, making presentations and recommendations to establish standards and practice guidelines as it relates to On-Campus Housing Residential Education; and interacting regularly with faculty, students, administration, parents, and other UC campuses.

The Director functions independently with minimal direction from the Executive Director, accepting challenging circumstances and taking initiative to solve problems where solutions are not readily apparent, interpreting established departmental, divisional, and university guidelines and policies, and prioritizing tasks while working in the midst of ever-changing student resident needs.

The Director partners closely with the Director of Residential Education, Apartment Housing, who oversees the department-wide Leadership & Involvement unit, to ensure that On-Campus Housing residents have full access to department-wide leadership development, civic engagement, and student governance programming. The Director also partners with the department’s Associate Director of Conduct to align case management, adjudication standards, and educational sanctioning across On-Campus Housing.

Director of Residential Education, Apartment Housing

About UCLA Residential Life

At UCLA Residential Life, we lead communities where every resident feels welcomed, connected, and supported. We serve more than 15,000 undergraduates in our residence halls and 12,000 undergraduate and graduate students in our apartments and family housing. Our work is carried out by 700+ student staff, 100 professional staff, and 20 Faculty-in-Residence, together making UCLA a place where community thrives.

Position Overview

The Director of Residential Education, Apartment Housing is a full-time, live-in professional responsible for implementing a comprehensive residential life program that encourages student engagement, academic success, and community building for the 12,000+ undergraduate, graduate, and family residents living in UCLA’s University Apartments North (UAN) and University Apartments South (UAS) communities. The Director plays a significant role in advancing UCLA’s mission, Student Affairs’ strategic initiatives, and the mission, vision, and values of the Office of Residential Life.

Reporting to the Executive Director of Residential Life and First Year Experience, the Director of Residential Education, Apartment Housing provides senior-level leadership for Residential Education programs and services across UCLA’s apartment-style housing. Residential Education staff in Apartment Housing, who report to the Director, provide a broad range of student affairs and student development functions tailored to the unique developmental needs of independent-living adult residents and resident families.

The Apartment Director leads across multiple geographic sites (University Apartments North, University Apartments South, and Family Housing) and serves three distinct populations (upper-division undergraduates, graduate students, and families with children), each with different developmental needs, conduct considerations, and partner ecosystems. This scope requires the Director to coordinate site-specific operations, tailor programs and policies to each population, and maintain strong relationships with graduate-serving units, family-serving partners, and apartment-style housing operations.

The Director is responsible to respond to life-threatening emergencies and after-hours crises and will live on-site in University Apartments and oversee all emergency and crisis response of the Apartment Housing Residential Education Team. The Director serves as a primary administrator-on-call within a department rotation, providing 24/7 emergency response coordination through an on-call system in partnership with the Director of Residential Education, On-Campus Housing.

Serving as a member of the Residential Life Executive Leadership Team, the Director functions with a high degree of autonomy in multiple roles, and is primarily responsible for independently exercising authority and making decisions in regard to Apartment Housing Residential Education based on broad goals established collaboratively with the Executive Director and peers in the department.

The Director is responsible for: strategic planning and implementation of an integrated Residential Education program serving Apartment Housing’s upper-division undergraduate, graduate, and family populations across multiple geographic sites; direct supervision of 4 full-time Assistant Directors (3 Residential Education and 1 Leadership & Involvement) who are formally evaluated annually; coordinating the efforts of the UAN and UAS areas and integrating Student Conduct, Leadership and Involvement, and graduate-student-serving units within Apartment Housing; overseeing an Apartment Housing Residential Education and Leadership & Involvement budget of approximately $3 million; performing management-level assignments for Apartment Housing, including, but not limited to, human resource and performance management, chairing and representing the department on a variety of standing and ad-hoc committees, developing operational enhancements, research analysis, making presentations and recommendations to establish standards and practice guidelines as it relates to Apartment Housing Residential Education and Leadership & Involvement; and interacting regularly with faculty, students, administration, families, and other UC campuses.

The Director functions independently with minimal direction from the Executive Director, accepting challenging circumstances and taking initiative to solve problems where solutions are not readily apparent, interpreting established departmental, divisional, and university guidelines and policies, and prioritizing tasks while working in the midst of ever-changing resident needs across a geographically dispersed apartment community.

The Director partners closely with the Director of Residential Education, On-Campus Housing on shared training, hiring cycles, crisis coverage, and cross-area student experience, and partners with the department’s Associate Director of Conduct to align case management, adjudication standards, and educational sanctioning across Apartment Housing.
